Conclusions

  • The bigger a house, the better grade in the King County scale but actually the biggest houses have condition marked as 3. (boxplots assumption)

  • Houses with 3 bedrooms seems to be a potential investment because of multiple outliers in price, while houses with 2 bedrooms seems to be a safe option while purchasing - low risk of overpaying. (boxplot assumption)

  • There is the biggest number of offers nearby 9716 Fremont Avenue North in Seattle. (bin2d assumption)

  • There is an interesting offer of a house where square footage without basement is more than 4500 and the price is less than 100000$. (bin2d assumption)

  • There are a few weak Pearson’s correlations but they do not describe surprising outcomes. For example, it was rather obvious that sqft_living and sqft_above or zipcode and long are quite correlated. Grade and square footage of a house seems to be correlated so the conclusion might be that the King County scale of gradation underestimate smaller houses and they could be differentials for investors in Seattle.
